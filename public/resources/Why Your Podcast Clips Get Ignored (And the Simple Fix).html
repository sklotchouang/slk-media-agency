The Truth About Ignored Clips

Sarah records a 60-minute podcast every week. Great guests. Amazing insights. She even pays someone to cut clips.

But her clips get 30-50 views. Sometimes less.

Meanwhile, someone with a smaller show gets thousands of views on every clip.

The problem isn't your content. It's not your audience. It's not even the platform.

The problem is how your clips are made and shared.

Problem #1: Your Clips Have No Hook

What Most People Do Wrong:

• Start clips in the middle of a thought

• Use boring opening lines like "So, um..."

• Give away the ending in the first 3 seconds

The Simple Fix:

Start every clip with a hook that creates curiosity. Your first sentence should make people think "I need to hear this."

Examples of Good Hooks:

❌ "I think social media is important..."

✅ "I made $100K from one Instagram post. Here's how..."

❌ "Let me tell you about my business..."

✅ "This mistake cost me $50,000. Don't make it."

Problem #2: Your Clips Are Too Long

What Most People Do Wrong:

• Make 2-3 minute clips (way too long for social media)

• Include too much setup and context

• Don't edit out the "ums" and pauses

The Simple Fix:

Keep clips between 30-90 seconds. Cut out everything that doesn't add value. Make every second count.

Perfect Clip Length by Platform:

📱 TikTok: 30-60 seconds

📸 Instagram Reels: 30-90 seconds

▶️ YouTube Shorts: 30-60 seconds

💼 LinkedIn: 60-90 seconds

Problem #3: Bad Visual Setup

What Most People Do Wrong:

• Use horizontal video on vertical platforms

• Poor lighting that makes you look unprofessional

• Boring background or messy room

• No captions (80% watch on mute!)

The Simple Fix:

Film in vertical format. Get good lighting. Clean up your background. Always add captions.

Quick Visual Checklist:

✅ Vertical video (9:16 ratio)

✅ Face well-lit and centered

✅ Clean, simple background

✅ Large, easy-to-read captions

✅ Eye contact with camera

Problem #4: No Clear Purpose

What Most People Do Wrong:

• Cut random parts of the episode with no strategy

• Don't think about what the viewer will get from watching

• No call-to-action or next step

The Simple Fix:

Every clip should have one clear purpose. What do you want the viewer to learn, feel, or do?

Types of High-Performing Clips:

💡 Quick Tips: "Here's how to..."

🚫 Mistake Warnings: "Don't do this..."

📈 Success Stories: "This worked for me..."

🎯 Controversial Takes: "Everyone gets this wrong..."

❓ Question Hooks: "What if I told you..."

Problem #5: Posting Without Strategy

What Most People Do Wrong:

• Post at random times when they remember

• Use the same caption for every platform

• Don't engage with comments or shares

• Post once and never promote again

The Simple Fix:

Post consistently when your audience is online. Write captions that spark conversation. Engage with every comment.

Platform-Specific Best Times:

📱 TikTok: 6-10pm EST, Tuesday-Thursday

📸 Instagram: 11am-1pm, 7-9pm EST

▶️ YouTube: 2-4pm EST, weekends

💼 LinkedIn: 8-10am, Tuesday-Thursday

Your Action Plan

1 Audit Your Current Clips

Look at your last 10 clips. Which problems do you see? Start there.

2 Pick One Fix

Don't try to fix everything at once. Start with better hooks, then move to the next fix.

3 Test and Track

Try the new approach on 5 clips. Compare the views to your old clips. You'll see the difference.

4 Scale What Works

Once you find what gets views, do more of that. Double down on what works.
