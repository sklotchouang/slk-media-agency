The Viral Hook Generator

50 Copy-Paste Templates That Get Clicks

Stop guessing what makes people click. Use these proven hook formulas to grab attention and get your podcast content seen by thousands.

Why Your Hook Makes or Breaks Your Content

Your hook is the first 3-5 seconds of your video or the first line of your post. It decides if someone keeps watching or scrolls past.

❌ Bad Hook

"Welcome back to my podcast. Today we're going to talk about..."

✅ Good Hook

"This mistake cost me $50,000. Here's how to avoid it..."

How to Use This Guide

  • • Pick a hook that fits your message
  • • Replace the [brackets] with your own words
  • • Test different hooks for the same content
  • • Track which ones get the most engagement

Problem/Solution Hooks

Start with a problem your audience faces, then promise a solution.

Problem Hook #1

"This mistake is killing your [NICHE] growth (and how to fix it)"

Example: "This mistake is killing your podcast growth (and how to fix it)"

Problem Hook #2

"If you're struggling with [PROBLEM], watch this"

Example: "If you're struggling with low downloads, watch this"

Problem Hook #3

"Why your [THING] isn't working (3 reasons)"

Example: "Why your podcast clips aren't working (3 reasons)"

Problem Hook #4

"Stop doing [BAD THING]. Do this instead."

Example: "Stop posting random clips. Do this instead."

Problem Hook #5

"The #1 reason [TARGET AUDIENCE] fail at [GOAL]"

Example: "The #1 reason podcasters fail at growth"

Curiosity Hook #2

Example: "What nobody tells you about podcast sponsorships"

Curiosity Hook #3

Example: "I discovered something weird about viral clips"

Curiosity Hook #4

Example: "Here's what happened when I posted 30 clips in 30 days"

Curiosity Hook #5

"The weird trick that [RESULT]"

Example: "The weird trick that doubled my downloads"

Number Hook #4

Number Hook #5

Example: "From 50 to 50,000 downloads: My podcast growth journey"

5 Quick Tips for Better Hooks

1

Test Multiple Hooks

Try 3-5 different hooks for the same content. See which gets the most engagement.

2

Keep It Short

Your hook should be 5-8 words max. Longer hooks lose impact.

3

Match Your Audience

Use words and problems your specific audience relates to.

4

Create Curiosity Gaps

Give just enough info to make them want more, but not everything.

5

Track What Works

Keep notes on which hooks get the best results for your content.

Bonus: Platform-Specific Hook Tips

Instagram/TikTok

  • • Use trendy language
  • • Add emotional words
  • • Keep it super short
  • • Use emojis sparingly

LinkedIn

  • • Focus on business results
  • • Use professional language
  • • Include numbers/data
  • • Target career growth

YouTube

  • • Can be slightly longer
  • • Focus on education
  • • Use "How to" often
  • • Promise specific outcomes
