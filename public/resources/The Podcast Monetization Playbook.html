How to Get Your First Sponsor (Even with 100 Listeners)

The Secret Most Podcasters Don't Know

Sponsors don't just care about listener numbers. They care about engagement, audience quality, and how well you can sell their product. A podcast with 500 engaged listeners can charge more than one with 5,000 passive listeners.

Before You Reach Out:

Know Your Numbers

Average downloads, listener demographics, engagement rates

Create a Media Kit

One-page PDF with your stats and audience info

Use Products You Love

Authentic recommendations work better than random sponsors

The Perfect Sponsor Pitch:

Email Template:

Subject: Partnership opportunity - [Your Podcast Name]

Hi [Name],

I love [specific product/feature]. I've been recommending it to my audience anyway.

My podcast [name] gets [X] downloads per episode with [audience description].

Would you be interested in a partnership? I could create authentic content about [product].

Happy to send my media kit if you're interested.

Best, [Your name]

$1.00-$2.00
Per download for dedicated episodes

The Psychology of Podcast Pricing

Pricing Mistakes That Kill Sales

  • Too cheap: Makes people think it's low quality
  • Round numbers: $100 feels less thought-out than $97
  • No payment plans: High prices with no options
  • No urgency: "Available forever" doesn't motivate action

Pricing Strategies That Work

  • Price for value: What result are you helping them achieve?
  • Odd numbers: $97, $197, $497 feel more strategic
  • Multiple options: Give people choices
  • Limited time offers: Create real urgency

The Magic Pricing Formula:

Calculate the Problem Cost

How much does this problem cost them monthly?

Factor in Time Savings

How many hours will you save them?

Price for Outcome

Charge 10-20% of the value you create

Example Calculation:

Problem costs them $5,000/month + You save them 20 hours/month ($2,000 value) = $7,000 value

Your price: $997-$1,497 (14-21% of value created)

7 Monetization Mistakes That Cost You Money

Mistake #1: Waiting Too Long

Thinking you need 10,000 downloads before you can make money.

Fix: Start monetizing from episode 1 with affiliate links and email signup.

Mistake #2: Only One Revenue Stream

Putting all your eggs in one basket (like only sponsorships).

Fix: Build 3-5 different income sources for stability.

Mistake #3: Underpricing Yourself

Charging $50 for something that saves people $5,000.

Fix: Price based on value created, not time spent.

Mistake #4: No Email List

Relying only on podcast platforms you don't control.

Fix: Mention your email list in every episode with a clear reason to join.

Mistake #5: Being Too Salesy

Every episode sounds like a commercial for your stuff.

Fix: Follow the 80/20 rule - 80% value, 20% promotion.

Mistake #6: No Clear Next Step

Listeners don't know what to do after the episode ends.

Fix: End every episode with one specific action step.

Mistake #7: Giving Up Too Soon

Expecting overnight success and quitting after 3 months.

Fix: Commit to at least 12 months and track small wins along the way.

The Bottom Line

Most monetization failures come from impatience and fear. Start small, be consistent, and focus on helping people. The money will follow.
