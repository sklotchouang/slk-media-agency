The 5-Step Content Multiplication System

1

Listen Like a Content Hunter

Don't just listen to your episode - hunt for golden moments. These are the parts that make people stop scrolling and pay attention.

What to Look For:

Quotable Moments

One-liners that pack a punch

Controversial Takes

Opinions that spark discussion

Teaching Moments

Quick tips or explanations

Personal Stories

Vulnerable or relatable moments

Pro Tip:

Keep a notebook while listening. Write down the timestamp and a few words about each golden moment.

2

Sort Your Content Gold

Not all content works the same way. Sort your golden moments into buckets based on how you'll use them.

Video Clips

Best quotes, teaching moments, stories

Use for: Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts

Quote Cards

One-liners, key insights, controversial takes

Use for: Instagram posts, LinkedIn

Carousels

Step-by-step processes, multiple tips

Use for: Instagram, LinkedIn carousels

Example from a Business Podcast:

Video Clip: "The biggest mistake I see entrepreneurs make is..." (2-minute explanation)

Quote Card: "Revenue without profit is just expensive hobbies"

Carousel: "5 Signs Your Business Model is Broken" (guest shared 5 specific signs)

3

Plan Your Content Week

One episode should give you content for 7-10 days. Here's how to space it out so you never run dry.

The Weekly Content Recipe:
M

Monday: Post the full episode + teaser quote card

T

Tuesday: Best video clip from the episode

W

Wednesday: Behind-the-scenes or personal story clip

T

Thursday: Educational carousel or tip post

F

Friday: Controversial take or discussion starter

S

Saturday: Quote card or quick tip

S

Sunday: Reflection post or audience question

The Magic Happens Here:

Instead of one episode getting 1 week of attention, your content now works for you all month long. Each post drives people back to the full episode.

4

Batch Your Content Creation

Don't create content one post at a time. That's how you end up feeling overwhelmed. Instead, batch everything in one session.

The 2-Hour Batch Session:
1

Listen & Mark (30 minutes)

Go through your episode and mark all the golden moments

2

Create Video Clips (45 minutes)

Edit 3-4 short video clips for the week

3

Design Quote Cards (30 minutes)

Use Canva to create 5-6 quote graphics

4

Write Captions (15 minutes)

Batch write all your social media captions

Result:

You now have 7-10 pieces of content ready to go. No more daily stress about "what should I post today?"

5

Schedule & Track Your Success

The final step is putting your content on autopilot and watching what works best.

Scheduling Tools
  • • Later (great for visual content)
  • • Buffer (simple and reliable)
  • • Hootsuite (all-in-one solution)
  • • Creator Studio (free for Instagram/Facebook)
What to Track
  • • Which clips get the most views
  • • Which posts drive podcast downloads
  • • What time your audience is most active
  • • Which content gets the most engagement
Success Looks Like This:

After 30 days, you'll have posted 30+ pieces of content from just 4 episodes. Your audience will see you everywhere, and your podcast downloads will grow consistently.

5 Mistakes That Kill Your Content Strategy

Mistake #1: Posting Random Clips

Just cutting random 60-second clips and hoping something sticks.

Fix: Choose clips with clear hooks and valuable takeaways.

Mistake #2: No Call-to-Action

Posting great content but never telling people what to do next.

Fix: Every post should drive people to listen to the full episode.

Mistake #3: Same Content, All Platforms

Using the exact same post for LinkedIn, Instagram, and TikTok.

Fix: Adapt your content for each platform's audience and format.

Mistake #4: Poor Audio Quality

Posting clips with background noise, echo, or low volume.

Fix: Clean up your audio before turning it into clips.

Mistake #5: No Consistent Branding

Every post looks different - different fonts, colors, and styles.

Fix: Create templates and stick to your brand colors and fonts.
